# Non-Goals

ProjectDoc Local should stay disciplined about what it is not trying to be, especially in early phases.

## Not a Fully Autonomous Back Office

The product is not intended to replace human judgment for compliance, payment, or contractual decisions. Automation should reduce clerical work, not eliminate accountable review.

## Not a General "Chat With Your Documents" Tool

The product is focused on structured intake, extraction, review, and workflow support for operational documents. Open-ended conversational document experiences are not the center of the product strategy.

## Not a Replacement for ERP, Accounting, or PM Systems

ProjectDoc Local should improve the quality and control of document intake before data reaches systems of record. It should not attempt to become the entire project management or accounting platform.

## Not a Clause-Level Contract Intelligence Suite in the MVP

Contracts matter, but deep legal analysis, redlining, and clause negotiation are separate product problems. Early versions should stay focused on operational document handling.

## Not a Cloud-First Multi-Tenant Product

The initial product direction is local deployment and customer control. A hosted offering may become relevant later, but it should not drive early product tradeoffs.

## Not a Promise of Perfect Extraction

The product should not be positioned around unrealistic accuracy claims. Confidence-aware workflows, clear exceptions, and reviewability are more important than inflated automation messaging.

## Not a Dumping Ground for Every Document Type

Supporting more document types only helps if the workflows stay reliable. New document families should be added deliberately, with clear operational value and test coverage.
